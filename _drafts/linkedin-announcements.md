## Post 2: Why Web 4.0 Might Succeed Where Web 3 Failed

The web made everything available. It also made everything your job.

Six tabs to compare flights. Temporary expertise in insurance fine print. Job boards that force you to think in dropdowns when you think "senior engineering role, remote-friendly, interesting product, not adtech."

Web 3 tried to fix the internet with ownership. I think the bigger problem was always effort. Web 4.0 starts there.

The self-service era offloaded work from paid intermediaries onto you — the unpaid user. AI agents invert that back. Describe what you want, review the options, confirm. Like having a travel agent or insurance broker, but at software cost.

And here's what I think is underappreciated: it's not just convenience. It changes who gets to participate. Your grandmother who can't navigate Booking.com. Someone navigating a new country's bureaucracy in a language they're still learning. People who are digitally literate enough to have a conversation but not to operate a comparison tool.

That's not a UX improvement. That's a different answer to who technology is for.

## Post 3: The Supply Side of Web 4.0

Does your product's value survive if the user never sees your UI?

That's the question I keep coming back to. If AI agents handle the interaction layer, what's left?

Some products die — thin wrappers over data that exists elsewhere. An agent does that natively. Some get commoditised — real capability persists, but as infrastructure the agent calls into. Salesforce still holds the data. You just stop using the interface. Some are untouched — where the interaction IS the product. Figma. Ableton. Excel when you're actually thinking in cells.

The uncomfortable part for those of us who build software: the same transition that creates demand (building MCP servers, trust infrastructure, API-first migrations) also arrives with a productivity multiplier that means fewer people do that work.

The SaaS era was the golden age of building interfaces. What comes next rewards capability, not chrome.
